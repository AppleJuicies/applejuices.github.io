Flow Simulation of Muffler
  • I am in charge of developing the muffler for my FSAE team, and there are a lot of designs that are viable but idealy we have something efficient and easy to manufacture. A large step and important piece of building the muffer is the size of the chamber that make up the destructive interference. In order to have an idea of where we want the chambers to sit, an idea I had was simulating air flow through the muffler and seeing which configurating reduces and restricts air flow the best. The aim here, assuming all parameters are constant (air pressure, velocity, etc.) was to see changes in the air flow. The more restrictive, the more the sound waves have to bounce around. Though this is OBVIOUSLY not the best way to simulate sound due to sound properties being ultimately different. The idea and execution was there and did help us get an idea and comparative during the fabrication of the new muffler.
